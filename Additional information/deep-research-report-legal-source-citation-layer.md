# CasePass Legal Source Citation Layer for Civil Handoffs in England and Wales

## Core findings

For a minimum viable CasePass deployment in England & Wales, the safest primary corpus is the official CPR web corpus on Justice UK, official legislation on legislation.gov.uk, official HMCTS and GOV.UK form pages, and regulator guidance from the SRA, BSB, Law Society, HMCTS, and ICO. Those sources are sufficient to ground the core litigation moments that matter in a civil handoff product: issue of claim, service, acknowledgment of service, defence, directions questionnaires, applications, disclosure, witness evidence, experts, hearings, costs, changes of representation, and appeals. citeturn9view1turn9view2turn9view3turn12view2turn15view1turn15view2turn15view3turn16view0turn22view1turn22view2

The product should not treat all forums as CPR-governed. The Business and Property Courts sit within the CPR civil courts architecture, but Employment Tribunals and the Upper Tribunal operate as separate jurisdictions with their own procedural rules and guidance. That means CasePass needs a forum-classification gate before any downstream validation, deadline extraction, or question-asking by the AI agent. citeturn25search7turn25search3turn7search11turn7search7turn7search6turn7search10turn7search19

A second design conclusion is that the handoff engine must distinguish between work that informs litigation and work that amounts to a reserved legal activity. Reserved activities, including conduct of litigation and rights of audience, are governed by statute and professional rules; a handoff tool can support preparation, metadata validation, and source-backed guidance, but it must flag where a receiving lawyer needs the right authorisation before adopting the conduct of the matter. citeturn31search0turn22view0turn35search5turn37view3turn32search2

A third design conclusion is compliance by design. Any AI workflow that ingests a case file, proposes questions, stores notes, and returns an updated brief necessarily touches confidentiality, supervision, conflicts, customer due diligence, lawful basis, and data minimisation. Those cannot be bolted on later; they must appear as workflow gates in the handoff itself. citeturn30search6turn30search3turn31search5turn28search5turn29search11turn29search1turn29search2turn30search5turn29search3turn30search16

## Legal Source Citation Layer design

### Functional architecture

The recommended MVP is a seven-step pipeline. First, a **source registry** stores canonical records for each official authority, document family, and citation convention. Second, **document ingestion** pulls HTML pages and PDFs from whitelisted official domains only. Third, a **text extraction layer** stores page-aware text for PDFs and section-aware text for HTML. Fourth, a **chunking layer** splits content by rule, paragraph, section, page, or form field. Fifth, a **citation resolver** maps any answer span to a source chunk with a pinpoint. Sixth, an **answer-grounding service** forces the chatbot to cite only retrieved primary chunks unless a human reviewer overrides. Seventh, a **verification layer** records confidence, forum certainty, and whether a solicitor has approved the answer. This structure is justified by the procedural granularity of the CPR, the forum split between CPR courts and tribunals, the strict rules on communications with the court, the regulation of representation changes, and the confidentiality and data-minimisation duties imposed on legal practice. citeturn12view2turn16view0turn22view0turn25search2turn7search11turn30search6turn29search2turn30search16

In practical product terms, the AI agent should answer only from three tiers. **Tier A** is primary law and primary procedure: CPR, practice directions, legislation, official forms, official court guides. **Tier B** is regulator guidance: SRA, BSB, Law Society, HMCTS, ICO. **Tier C** is internal inference: CasePass workflow policy, deadline heuristics, and schema mappings. Only Tier A and Tier B should appear as legal citations. Tier C should be labeled as “CasePass policy inference” or “needs solicitor review.” That distinction matters because not every procedural recommendation is the same thing as a legal rule. citeturn30search13turn30search9turn29search3turn26search10

### Citation rules

Every legal answer produced by CasePass should return six visible elements: **source**, **document name**, **URL**, **pinpoint** by rule/paragraph/page, **short excerpt or faithful paraphrase**, and **confidence level**. Where the answer is inferential rather than stated, the UI should say **“AI inferred from cited sources; solicitor review recommended.”** Where only regulator guidance exists and no primary rule or statute has been found, the UI should say **“No primary source found; guidance-level answer only.”** That standard matches the user’s need for traceability and is strongly aligned with the procedural need to avoid uncopied communications, evidence distortion, representation confusion, and uncontrolled data sharing. citeturn16view0turn30search2turn30search6turn30search5turn29search2

A concise output template for the assistant can look like this:

```text
Answer: The defence is usually due 28 days after service of particulars if an acknowledgment of service has been filed.
Source: Justice UK
Document: CPR Part 15 – Defence and Reply
URL: https://www.justice.gov.uk/courts/procedure-rules/civil/rules/part15
Pinpoint: r.15.4(1)(b)
Excerpt: “...28 days after service of the particulars of claim.”
Confidence: High
Status: Verified primary source
```

### Rejection rules

CasePass should refuse or downgrade answers in six common situations. It should refuse a definitive legal answer where no primary source can be found, where the only available source is secondary, where the uploaded document cannot be confidently assigned to a forum or ruleset, where the answer would require strategic legal judgment rather than source retrieval, where sources conflict, or where the system is being asked to attribute reserved-activity authority that it cannot verify. Those refusal rules are especially important because the applicable rules change materially across CPR courts, BPC specialist practice, tribunals, and regulated-profession boundaries. citeturn25search2turn7search11turn7search6turn31search0turn35search5

### Suggested user experience

The safest UX is a dual-pane answer view. The main answer should show inline citations such as **“CPR Part 15, r.15.4(1)(b)”**. A right-hand source panel should show the full document name, a clickable URL, the exact pinpoint, and a highlighted excerpt. For PDFs, the button should read **“Open PDF at cited page.”** Each answer should also display one of four badges: **Verified primary source**, **Regulator guidance**, **AI inferred – needs review**, or **Unverified / forum uncertain**. That is the cleanest way to translate hard procedural provenance into an interface a receiving solicitor can trust during a handoff. citeturn29search3turn30search16turn30search6turn16view0

### Application to the current CasePass workflow

In the current CasePass concept, the legal citation layer should connect at five points. During **file ingestion**, it classifies the forum, document type, and active rule family. During **chatbot questioning**, it restricts answers to source-backed questions, such as service validity, deadlines, track, disclosure stage, or hearing restrictions. During **handoff acceptance**, it checks reserved-activity and record-of-representation issues, including whether N434 / Part 42 action is needed. During the **hearing note workflow**, it forces a recording warning and channels transcript requests into the official workflow rather than any unofficial capture. During **post-action verification**, it requires the updating lawyer to link each procedural update to at least one cited source or filed document. This is the narrowest design that still respects CPR communications rules, hearing-recording prohibitions, representation changes, and professional-data obligations. citeturn16view0turn22view0turn35search5turn26search10turn30search6turn29search2
